Research Consent Form

Researcher: Jacob William Keith Ritchie

Affiliation: Department of Computer Science, University of Toronto

You are invited to participate in a research study investigating the perception of interactive bar charts. This research encompasses a questionnaire that will evaluate your education level, visual ability, personality traits, level of familiarity with charts, and your ability to answer questions using static or interactive bar charts.

Your decision to participate is voluntary and you are free to withdraw at any time without any consequences. You are not required to answer any questions you do not feel comfortable answering.

Upon completion of the experiment, you will be given a participant ID to submit on the Amazon Mechanical Turk website. You can withdraw from the study either by not submitting your participant ID, or by emailing the researchers with your participant ID and requesting to withdraw. In both cases your data will be destroyed and not included in the published results of the research study. After results are made public, anonymized data may be shared publicly and you will no longer be able to withdraw from the study.

By submitting this form, you indicate that you understand the following:

  • The purpose of the study is to understand the effects of interactivity on perception of bar charts;
  • All data that are shown in the charts included in the study are artificial. Any locations referenced are purely fictional;
  • I will be asked to complete a questionnaire. I can expect the questionnaire to take approximately 5 minutes to complete;
  • I agree to have my questionnaire responses recorded, as well as the amount of time spent using any interactive elements in the questionnaire;
  • I agree to have my monitor resolution, operating system version and web browser version recorded;
  • The researchers do not foresee any risks or stresses beyond what one might experience in a typical day of work;
  • I will be compensated $0.90 (USD) for the session;
  • I am free to withdraw the study at any time before the results of the study are made public (even after the completion of the study) and if I do wish so, the researcher will also destroy all respective data collected insofar;
  • All data collected in this study will be kept secure. Survey responses will be stored in a secure device. Only the researchers involved in this study will have access. In all data files, my responses will be identified with a code in order to preserve confidentiality. All data containing potentially identifying information will be destroyed 3 years after publication of results;
