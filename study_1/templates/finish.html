In this experiment you were shown a bar graph with a "cut axis" (also called a "truncated axis") - that is, the y-axis of the graph did not start at 0. You can see the effect of this by looking at the two graphs below.

No Cut Axis. Y-axis starts at 0.00%

Cut Axis. Y-axis starts at 81.00%

This kind of chart is known to be misleading, since it can make the difference between two bars on a chart appear larger than the difference in the numbers used to create the chart.

We wanted to test whether showing people both charts would change their perception of the difference in access to safe drinking water between two towns. You may have seen both of the charts above, or only one of them.

Willowtown and Silvatown are both fictional , and the numbers on the chart are artificial .

It is always a good idea to carefully check the numbers on the axes of any chart that you read.

If you wish to read more about this topic, you can consult the following resources:

  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Misleading_graph
  • Darrell Huff's book How to Lie with Statistics, Penguin, 1954 (ISBN 0-14-013629-0)
